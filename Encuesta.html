The Students' Survey Data

Twelfth grade students conducted a survey to assess primary, middle and upper school students' experience with online classes.

There were a total of 22 responses, with a varied demographic distribution: 18.18% from 11th grade, 22.73% from 12th grade, 27.27% from 6th grade, 4.55% from 7th grade, 18.18% from 8th grade and 9.09% from 9th grade. The main findings of the survey were:

Question 1: Do you sometimes feel left out from your classmates when there is no internet at school?

  • Yes: 40.9%
  • No: 27.3%
  • Sometimes: 31.8%

Question 2: How would you compare the quality of online classes to in-person classes?

  • Online: 4.5%
  • In-person: 40.9%
  • Neutral: 54.5%

Question 3: Have you encountered any accessibility issues with online course materials or platforms? (Like access to your online books, schedules, etc.)

  • Yes: 68.2%
  • No: 31.8%

Question 4: As a student do you feel as you receive enough personal/direct assistance and support from your teachers?

  • Yes: 59.1%
  • No: 40.9%

Question 5: Do you feel like you have the same level of education as in-person classes?

  • Yes: 59.1%
  • No: 40.9%
