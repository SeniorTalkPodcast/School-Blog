Cons of Online Classes

March 2, 2024

Blog Postcast Pros of Online Classes
The students' point of view on the disadvantages of online classes

The only cons regarding online classes are communication and internet issues, sometimes it is harder to understand a topic due to the school’s internet.

By: Victor Santos

Ease of distraction at home can hinder concentration and academic performance. Internet connection problems, both at home and at school, can disrupt the flow of the class and make active participation difficult. In addition, a lack of communication and attention from some teachers leaves us feeling disconnected and unsupported. Insufficient explanation of topics can lead to confusion and difficulties in understanding the concepts presented. Finally, teachers' tardiness in joining meetings generates frustration and delays our learning progress.

By: Yael Donis

Online classes can present internet connection problems, both on the part of the student and the educational institution, which can affect the quality of learning and lead to delays in the delivery of assignments. In addition, certain practical or interactive activities are exclusive to face-to-face classes, which limits the learning experiences of online students. The lack of face-to-face interaction can hinder the development of social relationships and active participation in the classroom, which can make some students feel more shy or disconnected. Finally, excessive screen time can have negative health consequences, such as eyestrain and sedentary lifestyles, raising concerns about the overall well-being of online learners.

By: Valeria Cifuentes

Some students may struggle because they do not have face-to-face interactions with their teachers and peers, which may hinder their engagement and understanding of the subject. Similarly, variables such as the digital divide or distractions at home can have a negative impact on the online learning experience.
